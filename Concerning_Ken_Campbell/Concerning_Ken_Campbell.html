Concerning Ken Campbell
by John Winsor, April 2009.
HOME

Ken Campbell was a successful comic actor and entertainer and is the only relative known to have attained national prominence.  He specialised in the "Theatre of the Absurd".  Ken was the son of Elsie Campbell née Handley, daughter of Walter John Handley who was half-brother to my grandfather Arthur Handley.  Hence Elsie Campbell was cousin to Dora and Daisy.

Ken was born in 1941 so he was four years younger than me.  His mother Elsie was born in 1905, one year before my own mother Daisy.  It appears that the family lived in Barkingside, Ilford where I attended secondary school, about two miles from our home.  It seems strange that his family and my own never contacted each-other over all those years.

During the 1970s my mother decided that she would like to meet Ken.  She found the address of his agent at Bromley library, then wrote to him via the agent.  Ken replied with a nice personal letter.  At that time he was living in Stoke Newington, north London.  But they never did meet.  My mother lived in hope that he might appear at the local theatre, but that never happened.

Sadly, Ken died suddenly in August 2008 aged 66.  He is survived by his ex-wife, the actress Prunella Gee, and daughter Daisy.
