Notification of Arthur's death
by John Winsor, April 2009.

9 / 9 / 17

Dear Mr. & Mrs. Handley,

I have here to perform the painful duty of informing you of your son Arthur's death.  He was killed on the 16th day of August in the Ypres sector.  I must admit that I have been a very considerable time informing you, but as he was transferred to another Company before hand, I had to make the necessary enquiries, and from all accounts he died bravely while performing his duties for his King & Country.

No 233320 Cpl. Ayton V.J. 3 Platoon
A. Company 1/2nd London Regt. R.J.

My note:  I am surprised that this formal statement was signed only by a Corporal and not by a commissioned officer.

12 / 9 / 17

Dear Mr. & Mrs. Handley,

I am writing you at the request of Dora, who expressed her wish in a letter dated 7 / 9 / 17 that I should convey full particulars as regards your son Arthur's death.

I have done my utmost to gain all information possible, and have found that poor Arthur was buried by a shell with another Sgt.  It occurred two hundred yards east of (censored) west of (censored).
