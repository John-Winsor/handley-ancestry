The Belle Époque in Ilford
by John Winsor, April 2009.		 HOME


Foreword: The "Belle Époque" in Europe is usually defined as that period between the beginning of the twentieth century and the start of the Great War.  It was seen in retrospect as a "Golden Age", a time of universal prosperity and happiness.   Although likely to be an exaggeration, this was surely the true "Belle Époque" for my grandparents and their children.


 


Arthur became the proprietor of a fish and poultry shop at 72 - 74 High Road, Ilford then in Essex.  The family lived above the shop.  I have an impression of his considerable commercial success and consequent prosperity.  It is obvious he had learned the basics of the business from his step-father Walter John who had owned a similar business in West Ham.

Arthur became a Freemason.  My grandmother used to tell me how Arthur would hold occasional Masonic meetings in their home.  She said that she could sometimes hear chanting and rhythmic handclapping.  That, of course, was the limit of her knowledge of the subject.  My mother used to have a jewel box containing Arthur's Masonic "jewels".  These were ingenious trinkets bearing mystic symbols.  I concluded that they were awarded in recognition of Arthur's generous contributions to Masonic charities.  Freemasonry appears to function at different levels and seems, here, to have served as an alternative "Chamber of Commerce".  It seems likely that Arthur's membership was an investment that would provide him with support at a future time of crisis as described in my article about the estrangement of his marriage and the collapse of his business in about 1922.

In 1906 was born Daisy Victoria who became my mother.  Dora and Daisy attended a Roman Catholic school in Ilford called "The Convent", although the family were never Catholic.

Arthur Thomas attended Ilford County High School, as did I.  Except that in my time the school had relocated to Barkingside, two miles away.  On the War Memorial in the vestibule was inscribed among other names:  "A. T. Handley".
