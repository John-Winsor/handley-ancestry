Can the Internet provide "permanent" Time Capsules?
by John Winsor, September 2022.
HOME

Foreword:

In creating this presentation I have often felt that my ancestors and their family members are not really dead.  Rather are they still alive and continue living in the past.  A paradoxical illusion hardly unique to myself.  It is as though the dead still retain a link to the living through heirlooms, letters, photographs and anecdotes.  To break the link is to consign them forever to oblivion, to non-existence until the end of time.


Since starting work on this Website in 2009 I have always intended to publish it as an Internet Time Capsule and historical resource for the benefit of present and future descendants of my maternal grandparents Arthur and Mary Handley.  But where would I find an organisation willing to host my Website in perpetuity and without payment?  Surely the nature of the Internet is ephemeral and fundamentally concerned with profit?

Usually this is true, but not always.  Recently I discovered two large Internet organisations called "Github" and "Netlify" both located in California.  They are willing to host small Websites for an indefinite period of time and free of charge.  This is a good example of American generosity for which I am truly grateful.

Time Capsule:    Stubbs Family Photos circa 1930s

It is a common experience that family photographs, if not mounted in albums, are allowed to accumulate in drawers or are stored in receptacles such as biscuit tins.  It is likely that such memorabilia would ultimately be consigned to the dustbin by someone lacking all interest in times past.
